Iphone 5

The iPhone 5 is Apple's sixth smartphone. It was manufactured by Foxconn (on contract). It was released on September 21, 2012 and discontinued on September 10, 2013. Its model names were A1428 (GSM), A1429 (CDMA) and A1442 (CDMA, China). The pricing started at $649. There was a shortage of screens at the time. Two million units in the initial 24 hours of pre-order; 5million units in the first three days. The dimensions of the iPhone 5 were 123.8 millimeters (4.87 inches) in Height, 58.6 millimeter (2.31 inches) in Width, 7.6 millimeters (0.30) in Depth, and the iPhone also had a mass of 112 grams. The iPhone 5 had a aluminum body lighter and thinner than the iPhone 4/3/2. The iPhone 5 has a 4-inch screen, .5 bigger than the previous iPhones. It also had a 16:9 aspect ratio, LTE support and a reinvented charger, as well as a Sony-made 8MP camera. iPhone received a bunch of generally positive feedback, there were many hardware issues that users didnâ€™t like (for example; the antenna that had to pointed in a certain direction). The iPhone 5 had a shorter lifespan compared to the 4 (4 years max.) The iPhone 5 uses iOS firmware for software unlike the iPhone 4 which used OS 4.0 firmware for software. The iPhone 5 was the first to be able to act as a hotspot for other cellular devices. The iPhone 5 was also the first to have Siri, A programmed computer to have to ability to do spoken commands.
